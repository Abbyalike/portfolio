Market Wrap: Stock fell, silver plunged, and the dollar gained, followed by Trump’s Fed nomination

By作者： Abby Qi • Jan. 30, 2026

U.S. stocks fell Friday, while gold and silver plunged, amid a dollar rally following President Donald Trump’s nomination of Kevin Warsh as the new Federal Reserve Chair.

The S&P 500 slipped 0.43% to 6939.03, its third straight down day. The tech-heavy Nasdaq fell 0.94% to 23461.82, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average pulled back 0.36% to finish at 48,892.47. The small-cap Russel 2000 dropped 1.55% to 2,613.74.

Despite Friday’s weakness, all major indexes recorded a positive January. The S&P 500 and Dow logged monthly gains of 1.4% and 1.7%, respectively, while the Russell 2000 jumped more than 5.3% for its biggest monthly gain since August. The S&P also closed higher for the week, backing the downtrend for the other benchmarks.

Spot silver plummeted 28% to $83.45, and spot gold tumbled 11% to $4713.90, both suffering their worst one-day slide since 1980. The sell-off in precious metals was driven by dollar strength.

The ICE USD Index rose 0.9%, its sharpest increase since May. The greenback's weakness since last year has driven one of the biggest bull runs in precious metal on record.

Known as an inflation hawk, Warsh warned of inflation risks from excessive easing while serving as Fed Governor from 2006-2011. He also spoke out against QE2 in 2010 when unemployment was still above 9%. The financial markets see lower odds of aggressive easing under Warsh's leadership, given his strong stance against inflation.

Trump said that he "have no doubt that he [Warsh] will go down as one of the GREAT Fed Chairmen, maybe the best," in a Truth Social post.

Treasury yields ended mixed, with the yield curve steepening. The two-year yield dropped 2.7 basis points to 3.524%, while the 10-year yield climbed 1.4 basis points to 4.241%.

Seven out of eleven sectors in the S&P finished in red, with materials and information technology leading the decline, down 1.8% and 1.3% respectively. Consumer staples and energy fared better, up 1.4% and close to 1%.

The S&P Material sector took the hardest hit on Friday, plunging 1.84% to 624.06. Leading the drop was Newmont Corp, the world’s largest gold miner, which slumped about 11%, directly tracking the massive decline in gold and silver.

Applovin plummeted 16.89% after Google released a competing VR game development tool called Project Genie. KLA Crop plunged over 15% as the semi equipment firm guided for slower growth in the current quarter. Western Digital tanked, as investors took profit despite stronger than expected result and upbeat guidance.

Apple edged up 0.46% as earnings best estimates on strong iPhone sales. But CEO Tim Cook warned that memory shortages could hurt future margins.

Deckers Outdoor, the parent company of UGG and HOKA, skyrocketed 19.46% after quarterly EPS of $3.33 beat consensus estimates, and the firm raising its fiscal year 2026 revenue guidance to approximately $5.4 billion.

Verizon Communications, the largest U.S. wireless carrier, surged 11.83% in its best day since 2008 after posting strong fourth-quarter earnings and a 2026 outlook that topped expectations. Major cable and broadband provider Charter Communications climbed 7.62% as it reported a smaller-than-expected loss of high-speed internet subscribers for the quarter.

The CBOE Volatility Index edged up 0.56 to 17.44. Decliners lead advancers by 18 to 12 among the 30 Dow components.
